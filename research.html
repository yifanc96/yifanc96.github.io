Research

I enjoy topics around the analysis and numerical aspects of scientific computing and scientific machine learning. The overarching goal is to leverage scientific models, such as domain-specific knowledge like partial differential equations (PDEs), combined with data analysis techniques in statistics, efficient algorithms from computer and computational science, and the power of automation through machine learning, to drive theoretical comprehension and practical effectiveness in mathematical computation and inference.

Some specific topics of study include:

  • Multiscale Methods in Scientific Computing: multiscale algorithms, model reduction
  • Randomization in Scientific Computing: randomized numerical linear algebra, experimental design, active learning
  • Gaussian Processes for Scientific Machine Learning: fast algorithms, uncertainty quantification
  • Statistical Sampling: variational inference, gradient flows, Kalman methodology, diffusion models
